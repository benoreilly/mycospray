Active Ingredients

BioSyn Hand Sanitizer is an American made, benzalkonium chloride (BZK) based sanitizer. BZK based sanitizers are scientifically proven to be more effective and less detrimental than alcohol based sanitizers.

The main difference between these two types of sanitizers is that once the alcohol evaporates off the hands, which takes about 2 minutes, it is no longer killing any germs. Our BZK-based formula has a sustained antimicrobial activity duration of two hours, helping to prevent accidental contamination through casual contact or by touching a contaminated surface.

Advantages

Alchohol-free hand sanitizers have several key advantages over those based in alcohol.

Alcohol-Free Hand Sanitizer

  • Non-flammable.
  • No hazmat paperwork needed.
  • Can be safely stored.
  • Dual functionality; acts as both a re-moisturizing hand sanitizer and as a wound care antiseptic killing 99.99% of germs on contact.
  • Safe for use in all environments – medical, schools, home, institutions, police departments, prisons, food processing, packaging and handling, transportation, retail & restaurants.
  • No volatile organic compounds (VOCs).
  • helps to protect the hand’s natural oil barrier while still reducing germs.
  • BioSyn HS active material is considered a wound care Antiseptic by the FDA.
  • The absence of excess scent or color additives ensure that users with breathing issues and/or sensitive skin are free from risk.
  • The thin and silky texture of alcohol-free sanitizers ensures product spreads easily and quickly on the hands.
  • Can be used on aircraft as it does not contain banned alcohol.

Alcohol-Based Hand Sanitizer

  • Highly Flammable.
  • Requires hazmat paperwork and other redtape.
  • Removes natural oil from the skin which causes dry & cracked hands and allows germs to more easily enter the bloodstream through the skin.
  • Can cause adverse skin conditions including dermatitis which weakens the skin’s natural barrier against germs.
  • Alcohol Poisoning; a sanitizer containing 70% alcohol by volume is equal to 140-proof. Especially detrimental to kids and can lead to alcohol toxicity if overused.
  • If hands are dirty or grimy washing them is a necessity before applying.
  • Alcohol “odor”; users complaining of sickening smell of alcohol while having to apply it very frequently
  • Minimal efficacy duration; study data and reports support that once dry, effectiveness only lasts 2 minutes.
  • Alcohol-based sanitizers are prohibited on aiplanes, in prisons, schools, police precincts, etc.

Case Study

Alcohol Presence In The Blood From Using Alcohol Based Hand Sanitizers

Published By The Journal of Analytical Toxicology

Conclusions:  Intensive exposure to ethanol containing hand sanitizer can produce urinary EtG concentrations in excess of 2000 ng /mL, or two-to four-fold greater than commonly accepted threshold concentrations used to identify ethanol consumption. False accusations of ethanol consumption can have catastrophic consequences for individuals being monitored in programs that require abstinence. The urinary EtG concentrations that may occur with intensive dermal exposure suggest that moderate exposures are also likely to yield urinary EtG concentrations that exceed 500 ng /mL . These data, which reflect urinary ethanol metabolite concentrations under circumstances that simulate reasonable scenarios for healthcare professionals who cleanse their hands between patient contacts, demonstrate that significant dermal (and/or respiratory) absorption of ethanol occurs, and that use of ethanol containing hand sanitizers can produce urinary concentrations of EtG that by current standards, may be interpreted as deliberate ethanol use. Limited data indicate that neither raw nor corrected EtS concentrations exceed 100 ng/mL with dermal ethanol exposure. Further work is needed to define the roles, ratios, and cutoff values necessary to make EtG and  EtS useful markers of ethanol exposure and consumption.
